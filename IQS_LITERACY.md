# iQ-Studio Literacy & Linguistic Standards

This document is an extension of `IQS.md`, focusing on the linguistic quality, professional tone, and nomenclature consistency of iQ-Studio documentation. High-quality documentation requires professional English and consistent naming conventions.

## 1. Nomenclature Integrity

Strictly use official product names with correct capitalization. Consistency is key for brand trust and technical clarity.

- **Computing Platforms**:
    - **Correct**: `NVIDIA AGX Orin`, `Qualcomm Dragonwing QCS9075`, `EXMP-Q911`, `Jetson AGX Orin`.
    - **Incorrect**: `nvidia AGX orin`, `nvidia agx thin`, `qcom`, `AGX ORIN`.
- **Hardware Components**:
    - **Correct**: `Hexagon HTP`, `UVC Camera`, `MIPI CSI-2`.
- **Software Entities**:
    - **Correct**: `iQ-Studio`, `iqs-launcher`, `OGenie`, `InnoPPE`, `Yocto Linux`.

## 2. Table & Status Grammar

Status columns and technical matrices must use consistent verb forms (typically past participles) to indicate state.

- **Status Indicators**:
    - **Correct**: `Supported`, `Not supported`, `Coming soon`.
    - **Incorrect**: `Support`, `Not support`, `Comming soon`.
- **Formatting**:
    - Ensure icons like ✅ or ❌ are followed by consistently capitalized text.

## 3. Typographical Hygiene

Clean typography prevents visual friction and maintains professionalism.

- **Space Management**:
    - Ensure exactly one space follows every period (`.`), semicolon (`;`), and colon (`:`), unless it is at the end of a line.
    - **Parentheses**: Always include a space before an opening parenthesis when it follows a word (e.g., `simultaneously (1 live UVC...)`).
- **Punctuation Standards**:
    - Scan for and remove redundant punctuation (e.g., `videos..` or `installation."`).
    - Use Oxford commas where it improves clarity in lists.

## 4. Phonetic & Semantic Polishing

Avoid "broken English" or awkward phrasing by focusing on direct, object-oriented language.

- **Direct Object References**:
    - **Better**: "You will see it on the screen" instead of "You will see in the screen".
- **Sentence Integrity**:
    - Avoid fragmented sentences. When setting a condition, ensure the consequence follows in the same sentence or a clear independent clause.
    - **Better**: "When you need to update the BSP or recover a system, you can refer to..." instead of splitting into a fragment.

## 5. Literacy Pass (Self-Audit)

Before submitting any documentation update, perform a mandatory "Literacy Pass":
1. **Search** for common typos: `Comming`, `mutil`, `Support` (in tables).
2. **Verify** capitalization of all brands (NVIDIA, Qualcomm, Innodisk).
3. **Check** for missing spaces after periods.
4. **Read** out loud to ensure the flow is natural and professional.
